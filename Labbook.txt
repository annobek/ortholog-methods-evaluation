### Bash script for parameter calculation (`get_halsynteny_parameters.sh`) vs the provided calculation

What the bash script does:
1. Calculates intergenic distances
2. Computes median from the distances
3. Calculates minBlockSize = median *2 and maxAnchorDistance=median*20
-> Uses fixed multipliers
-> Tends to give stricter (smaller) parameter values since it uses a median

Parameter calculation provided in the issue:
- Defines 90th percentile of intergenic distances as maxAnchorDistance (includes 90% of typical gene gaps)
- minBlockSize = 0.5 * maxAnchorDistance
-> The resulting parameters are larger and less stricter than from the script
-> Allows longer synteny blocks and fewer breaks
-> Likely more suitable in practice, since it scales directly with the actual distribution of intergenic distances and adapts to genome-specific characteristics

The studies do not mention explicitly 90th percentile of intergenic distances, but their recommendations imply a similar rationale:
- halSynteny parameters should be chosen based on genome characterisitcs (gene density, intergenic interval legnth, assembly quality)
- Synteny identification is easier in species with higher gene density and shorter intergenic regions (https://doi.org/10.1186/s12859-018-2026-4)
- In practice, maxAnchorDistance should be large enough to keep real neighboring genes in the same synteny block, but small enough to avoid merging across large non-conserved or gene-poor regions
- minBlockSize is chosen so that only real, biologically meaningful conserved regions are kept, while very short, accidental similarities are ignored
-> 50 kb parameters correspond typically ~80-90th percentile in mammals, so this should be enough for high-quality synteny regions

         print $10, $12, $13, block_id > bed_query
         print $14, $16, $17, block_id > bed_target
     }' "$psl_input"
        -> everything is under one awk

Interpretation of the fraction plot:
- The fraction tells us, how different are the N-scores of the candidates picked by Cactus and synteny neighborhood algorithm in a contradictory case.
- So if we have, for example N-score for Cactus-candidate 13 and for Neighborhood candidate 15 (so the candidates have similar neighborhood support), we get the fraction approx. 0.5.
- If we have 1 for Cactus-candidate and 8 for Neighborhood-candidate (so the neighborhood algorithm picked the one candidate with the stronger neighborhood support), we get the fraction 0.1.
- If Cactus-candidate has the stronger neighborhood support, as the Neighborhood-candidate (which we don't expect often, as far as I understood), we have the fraction >0.5 to 1.
- The plot tells us that we have in the most of the cases candidates with similar neighborhood support.

GRIS:
- GRIS: Geometric Reciprocal Identity-Span score (name was suggested by chatgpt)
  -> geometric mean of overlap and percent identity, so the same as geometric mean in synteny algorithm pipeline
  -> reciprocal because 
  -> identity-span because
  -> So GRIS literally measures how broadly (span) and how well (identity) two genes align.

Reciprocal — ideally means the score is symmetric between the two species (Canis ↔ Macaca).
In practice, a single-direction GRIS (Canis→Macaca) can differ slightly due to how alignments are exported, but the reciprocal GRIS (e.g., sum or mean of both directions) restores that symmetry — it’s “reciprocal” in the conceptual sense
